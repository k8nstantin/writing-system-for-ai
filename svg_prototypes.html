The Laws of Hollow Taxonomic Geometry

  • 1. The Hollow Polygon Mandate: We completely reject standard mathematical operators (+, -, ~) and literal pictograms (no hearts, houses, cogs, or trees). Every prime noun belongs to a pure, closed, hollow outline-only polygon.
  • 2. The Vertex-Connecting Line Rule: By keeping shapes hollow, we reserve their interiors strictly for drawing static, symmetric vertex-connecting lines (grids, diagonals, bisections). This defines specific sub-species (e.g. Square = Structure, Square + Grid = Database Table).
  • 3. The 4-Stage Modification Chain: Modifiers stack in a rigid, non-overlapping sequence: Base Shape ➔ ANTI Negation (★) ➔ ARCH Rotation (︵) ➔ COMPASS Vector (✦).
  • 4. The Dual-Arc Mechanics: Rotating arches touch flat Edges to indicate Stable States (nouns/adjectives) or sharp Vertices to indicate Dynamic Actions/Transformations (verbs).
  • 5. The Inward vs. Outward Vertex Theorem: On stellated polygons, Outward Vertices (Tips) represent Emission/Projection (giving/flowing out), and Inward Vertices (Valleys) represent Absorption/Contraction (consuming/flowing in).
  • 6. The Center Dot (·) Separator: A bold center dot · on the baseline represents a physical word space, keeping the sentence's geometric spine unbroken.
