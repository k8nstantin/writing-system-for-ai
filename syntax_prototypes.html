Solving the "Bracket Problem"

If this notation sits between programming (like Rust) and natural language, it cannot look like a dense abstract syntax tree. We must replace structural brackets with visual hierarchy and indentation.

The Target Sentence

"I want you to know this."

1. The Old Notation (Bracket Soup)

[ ⟿ agt:◉ rec:◎ res:[ ⊢ agt:◎ pat:▷ ] ]

Problem: The eye has to parse the syntax `[`, `]`, `:` before it can see the meaning. The brackets overpower the symbols.

2. The New Notation (Indentation & Subtle Borders)

Solution: Use YAML/Python-style indentation. The left border acts as the "bracket" connecting a prime to its roles. The roles are de-emphasized typography.

agt
rec
res
agt
pat ▷

3. The Linear "Pill" Notation (For simple sentences)

For simple subject-verb-object structures, we don't even need indentation. A pill container implicitly groups them left-to-right.

agt pat

"I do something."
